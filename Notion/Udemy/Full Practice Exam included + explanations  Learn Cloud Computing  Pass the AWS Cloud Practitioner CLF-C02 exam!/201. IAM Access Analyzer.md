**IAM Access Analyzer** helps you identify **AWS resources that are shared externally**, meaning resources that can be accessed from **outside your AWS account or organization**.

It's built **inside the IAM console**.

---

# **1. What Access Analyzer Detects**

It scans resource-based policies and finds external access for:

- **S3 buckets**
- **IAM roles** (trusted entities)
- **KMS keys**
- **Lambda functions & layers**
- **SQS queues**
- **Secrets Manager secrets**
- (General rule: resources with *resource policies*)

---

# **2. Zone of Trust**

You define your **trusted boundary**, which can be:

- Your **AWS account**
- Your **AWS Organization (all accounts under the org)**

Anything *outside the zone of trust* that has permissions to your resources becomes a **finding**.

---

# **3. Example**

You have an S3 bucket. Its policy allows access to:

- IAM Role (same account)
- User (same account)
- VPC endpoint (same account)
- **Another AWS account → external**
- **Public IP range → external**

If your zone of trust = *your account*,

then Access Analyzer will report:

- External AWS account → **finding**
- Public client / IP → **finding**

This helps you identify **unintended public or cross-account exposure**.

---

# **4. Why It Matters (Exam Focus)**

IAM Access Analyzer is used for:

✔ Detecting unintentional public S3 buckets

✔ Detecting IAM roles that trust external accounts

✔ Identifying KMS keys shared externally

✔ Finding Lambda layers/functions exposed

✔ Identifying SQS queues accessible by other accounts

Not used for permissions management — only **visibility & findings**.

---

# **5. How Findings Are Shown**

Findings appear directly in the IAM console, showing:

- What resource is exposed
- Who has access
- What permissions they have
- Whether it’s inside or outside the trust zone

You can then **remediate** by editing the resource policy.
