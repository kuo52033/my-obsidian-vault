## **What is AWS IoT Core?**

- **Managed service** to connect **Internet of Things (IoT) devices** to AWS
- Enables devices to **securely send, receive, and process data** in the cloud

---

## **What is IoT (Internet of Things)?**

A network of **internet-connected physical devices** that:

- Collect data
- Send data to the cloud
- Receive commands or updates

### **Examples of IoT Devices**

- Connected cars
- Smart lights
- Smart refrigerators
- Sensors (temperature, humidity, motion)
- Industrial machines

---

## **Core Capabilities of AWS IoT Core**

- Secure device connectivity
- Scalable messaging (supports **billions of devices**)
- Reliable communication even when devices are **intermittently offline**
- Acts as a **publish / subscribe (pub-sub) messaging broker**

---

## **Messaging Model**

- Devices **publish** messages (sensor data, status)
- Other devices or applications **subscribe** to topics
- Enables **device-to-device** and **device-to-cloud** communication

---

## **Security**

- Secure authentication of devices
- Encrypted communication
- Fine-grained access control for device actions

---

## **Offline Device Support**

- Applications can **communicate with devices even when they are offline**
- Messages can be processed or queued until devices reconnect

---

## **AWS Service Integrations**

AWS IoT Core integrates with:

- **AWS Lambda** → process IoT events
- **Amazon S3** → store device data
- **Amazon SageMaker** → ML on IoT data
- **Amazon DynamoDB** → store device state
- **Amazon CloudWatch** → monitoring & logging

---

## **What You Can Build with AWS IoT Core**

- Real-time monitoring systems
- Smart home applications
- Connected vehicle platforms
- Industrial IoT (IoT analytics & automation)
- Predictive maintenance solutions

---

## **What AWS IoT Core Is NOT**

- ❌ Not a general messaging queue like SQS
- ❌ Not a database
- ❌ Not a device manufacturer solution
- ❌ Not limited to small scale (built for massive scale)
