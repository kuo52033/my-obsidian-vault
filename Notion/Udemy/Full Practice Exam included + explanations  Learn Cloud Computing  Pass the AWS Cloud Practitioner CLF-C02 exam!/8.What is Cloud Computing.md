## ☁️ What is Cloud Computing?

The video defines cloud computing as the **on-demand delivery** of IT resources (like compute power, databases, and storage) through a cloud services platform.

The two most important concepts are:

1. **On-demand**: You get resources exactly when you need them, instantly (within seconds), without ordering in advance.
2. **Pay-as-you-go**: You only pay for what you use, when you use it. This is a major shift from traditional IT.

Instead of building and managing your own data center, you use the cloud provider's (like AWS) hardware and infrastructure, accessing it via a web application.

---

## Examples of Cloud Services

You already use the cloud, often without realizing it:

- **Gmail**: A cloud email service.
- **Dropbox/Google Drive**: Cloud storage services (Dropbox was originally built on AWS).
- **Netflix**: A cloud video-on-demand service built entirely on AWS.

---

## Types of Clouds (Deployment Models)

The video outlines three main types of clouds:

- **Private Cloud**: Cloud services used by a **single organization**. It is not public and offers high control and security.
- **Public Cloud**: Resources are owned and operated by a third-party provider (like **AWS**, Google Cloud, or Microsoft Azure) and delivered over the internet.
- **Hybrid Cloud**: A **mix** of private and public clouds. A company might keep sensitive data on its private cloud (on-premises) but use the public cloud for flexibility and cost-effectiveness.

## 🏷️ Five Characteristics of Cloud Computing

3. **On-demand self-service**: Users can provision resources (like servers) automatically without human intervention from the provider.
4. **Broad network access**: Resources are available over the network.
5. **Multi-tenancy and resource pooling**: Multiple customers securely share the same physical infrastructure, which is managed by the provider.
6. **Rapid elasticity and scalability**: You can quickly and automatically scale resources up or down based on demand.
7. **Measured service**: Your usage is measured, and you pay *only* for what you have used.

---

## 🏆 Six Advantages of Cloud Computing

8. **Trade Capital Expense (CapEx) for Operational Expense (OpEx)**: You stop paying upfront for physical hardware (CapEx) and instead rent resources as needed (OpEx).
9. **Benefit from massive economies of scale**: Because providers like AWS are so large, they are more efficient and can offer lower prices.
10. **Stop guessing capacity**: You no longer need to predict your future server needs. You can scale automatically based on actual usage.
11. **Increased speed and agility**: You can get resources in minutes, allowing you to develop and launch applications much faster.
12. **Stop spending money running and maintaining data centers**: You can focus on your applications and customers instead of managing hardware, power, and cooling.
13. **Go global in minutes**: You can easily deploy your application worldwide using the provider's global infrastructure.

### Problems Solved by the Cloud

In summary, the cloud makes your infrastructure more:

- **Flexible & Agile**
- **Cost-effective**
- **Scalable** (adding resources to meet demand)
- **Elastic** (scaling out and in as needed)
- **Highly Available & Fault-Tolerant** (relying on a global fleet of data centers)
