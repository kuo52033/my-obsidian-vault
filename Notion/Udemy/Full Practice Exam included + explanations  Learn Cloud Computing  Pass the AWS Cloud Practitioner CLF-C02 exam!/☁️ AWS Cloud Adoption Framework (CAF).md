**CLF-002 – Must Memorize**

---

## 🎯 What is AWS CAF? (Exam-friendly)

The **AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (CAF)** is a **whitepaper (not a service)** that helps organizations **plan and execute digital transformation** using AWS.

> Purpose:
> **Guide cloud adoption & transformation using AWS best practices**

---

## 🧩 Key Characteristics (Exam Facts)

- ❌ Not an AWS service
- ✅ A framework / ebook (whitepaper)
- Built from **AWS best practices** and **customer experience**
- Helps organizations:
    - Build a transformation strategy
    - Execute cloud adoption successfully

---

## 🧱 Core Structure of CAF (VERY IMPORTANT)

CAF is composed of **organizational capabilities**, grouped into **6 perspectives**

👉 **You MUST remember these 6 for the exam**

### ✅ The 6 CAF Perspectives (Memorize)

1. **Business**
2. **People**
3. **Governance**
4. **Platform**
5. **Security**
6. **Operations**

---

## 🧠 Perspective Grouping (High-Yield Exam Concept)

CAF perspectives are grouped into **2 categories**:

### 🧑‍💼 Business Capabilities

- **Business**
- **People**
- **Governance**

> ⭐ Exam favorite:
> **People = bridge between business and technology**

---

### 🛠️ Technical Capabilities

- **Platform**
- **Security**
- **Operations**

---

## 🧑‍💼 Business Capability Perspectives

### 1️⃣ Business Perspective

Ensures cloud investments:

- Support **business outcomes**
- Accelerate **digital transformation**

Examples of capabilities (FYI, not mandatory to memorize):

- Strategy management
- Portfolio management
- Product management
- Innovation
- Data monetization

---

### 2️⃣ People Perspective ⭐ (Exam Favorite)

Acts as a **bridge between technology and business**

Focus areas:

- Culture
- Leadership
- Organizational structure
- Workforce transformation
- Cloud fluency

Exam phrasing:

> Helps organizations evolve culture and enable continuous learning

---

### 3️⃣ Governance Perspective

Focuses on:

- Risk management
- Financial control
- Compliance
- Cloud financial management

Goal:

> Maximize benefits while minimizing transformation risks

---

## 🛠️ Technical Capability Perspectives

### 4️⃣ Platform Perspective

Builds a **scalable, enterprise-grade cloud platform**

Focus areas:

- Platform architecture
- Data architecture
- CI/CD
- Infrastructure provisioning
- Cloud-native application development

---

### 5️⃣ Security Perspective

Ensures:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

Includes:

- IAM
- Threat detection
- Data protection
- Incident response
- Security governance

---

### 6️⃣ Operations Perspective

Ensures cloud services:

- Meet business needs
- Run reliably over time

Includes:

- Monitoring & observability
- Incident & problem management
- Patch & configuration management
- Availability & continuity

---

## 🔄 Transformation Domains (Exam Content)

CAF also defines **4 transformation domains**

👉 These describe *what* is being transformed

### 1️⃣ Technology

- Migrate & modernize:
    - Infrastructure
    - Applications
    - Data & analytics

---

### 2️⃣ Process

- Digitize & automate operations
- Use data & analytics
- Apply machine learning for insights

---

### 3️⃣ Organization ⭐

- Reorganize teams around:
    - Products
    - Value streams
- Use **Agile methods**
- Enable fast iteration

> ⚠️ Common exam question area

---

### 4️⃣ Products

- Create new:
    - Products
    - Services
    - Revenue models

---

## 🚀 CAF Transformation Phases (MUST MEMORIZE)

There are **4 phases**, always in this order:

### 1️⃣ Envision

- Identify business opportunities
- Define how cloud accelerates outcomes

---

### 2️⃣ Align

- Assess against **6 CAF perspectives**
- Identify capability gaps
- Produce an **action plan**

---

### 3️⃣ Launch

- Build & deploy **pilot workloads**
- Deliver initial business value

---

### 4️⃣ Scale

- Expand pilots
- Achieve desired scale
- Realize full business benefits

---

## 🧠 CLF-002 Exam Tips (Very Important)

### You may be asked to:

- Identify **CAF perspectives**
- Match a **capability → correct perspective**
- Identify **transformation domains**
- Order or recognize **transformation phases**

### Elimination Strategy

Even if unsure:

- Security-related → **Security**
- Culture / skills → **People**
- Monitoring / incidents → **Operations**
- Financial control → **Governance**
