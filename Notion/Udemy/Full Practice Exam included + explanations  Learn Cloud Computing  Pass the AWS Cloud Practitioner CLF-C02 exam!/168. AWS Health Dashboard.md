AWS Health Dashboard has **two main parts**, serving different purposes.

---

## **1. Service History (formerly AWS Service Health Dashboard)**

Shows **global AWS service status** across all regions.

- Public information for everyone
- Shows outages or incidents for any AWS service
- Historical data available (daily view)
- Optional RSS feed subscription
- Does **NOT** reflect your specific account usage
- Example: “S3 in us-west-1 is degraded”

Think of it as AWS's “system-wide health page”.

---

## **2. Health Dashboard for Your Accounts**

(Previously **Personal Health Dashboard – PHD**)

This dashboard provides **account-specific** information.

### **Key Features**

- Shows issues **impacting your AWS resources directly**
- Alerts about:
    - Service degradation that affects your resources
    - Scheduled maintenance
    - Upcoming changes
- Provides *remediation guidance*
- Displays event logs of past incidents
- Can **aggregate health events** across your entire AWS Organization

### **Examples**

- EC2 issue impacting your specific instances
- RDS scheduled maintenance window for your DB
- IAM certificate rotation notice
- Lambda regional outage affecting your functions

# ⭐ **CLF-C02 Exam Tips**

### **1. Two dashboards, different purposes**

| Dashboard | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Service History** | Public AWS-wide service status (global events) |
| **Health Dashboard for Your Account** | Personalized impact on *your* resources |

→ Expect exam questions asking which one to use.

---

### **2. Keywords for Health Dashboard for Your Accounts**

Questions may refer to:

- “Events impacting **your resources**”
- “Personalized alerts”
- “Proactive notifications”
- “Scheduled maintenance affecting *your account*”

Correct answer:

✔ **AWS Health Dashboard for Your Account**

---

### **3. Organization-wide visibility**

If question refers to:

- “Aggregate health across all accounts”
- “Multi-account environment”

Answer:

✔ **Use Health Dashboard with AWS Organizations**

---

### **4. Not a monitoring service**

AWS Health Dashboard does **not**:

- Monitor CPU, logs, or metrics (CloudWatch does that)
- Show application-level health
- Generate alarms

It is specifically about **AWS service events** affecting you.
