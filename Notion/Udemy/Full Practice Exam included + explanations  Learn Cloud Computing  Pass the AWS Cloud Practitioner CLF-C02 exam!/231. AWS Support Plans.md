AWS offers **4 support plans**.

Exam questions often ask: *Which support plan is appropriate for a given scenario?*

---

## **1. Basic Support (Free)**

### **Features**

- 24×7 access to:
    - Customer service
    - AWS documentation
    - Whitepapers
    - Support forums
- **Trusted Advisor:** Only **7 core checks**
- **AWS Personal Health Dashboard**

### **Use Case**

- Learning AWS
- Non-production experiments
- No access to AWS engineers

---

## **2. Developer Support**

### **Features**

- Everything from Basic
- **Business-hours email access** to Cloud Support Associates
- Unlimited support cases / Unlimited contacts

### **Response Times**

- **General guidance:** < 24 business hours
- **System impaired:** < 12 business hours

### **Use Case**

- Early dev environments
- Non-critical workloads
- Developers needing occasional AWS assistance

---

## **3. Business Support**

**Required for production workloads.**

### **Features**

- Everything from Developer
- **24×7 phone, email, and chat** with Cloud Support Engineers
- **Full Trusted Advisor checks + API access**
- **Infrastructure Event Management (IEM)** – extra fee

### **Response Times**

- General guidance: < 24 hours
- System impaired: < 12 hours
- **Production system impaired:** < 4 hours
- **Production system down:** < 1 hour

### **Use Case**

- Services running in production
- Need fast support + proactive guidance
- Companies needing full Trusted Advisor checks

---

## **4. Enterprise On-Ramp Support**

(Intended for **production or business-critical** workloads)

### **Features**

- Everything from Business
- Access to a **pool of Technical Account Managers (TAMs)**
- **Concierge team** for billing & best practices
- **Operations Reviews:**
    - Well-Architected review
    - Infrastructure Event Management
    - Operations assessment

### **Response Times**

- Production impaired: < 4 hours
- Production system down: < 1 hour
- **Business-critical system down:** < 30 minutes

### **Use Case**

- Large-scale production workloads
- Need operational reviews
- Preparing for major product launches

---

## **5. Enterprise Support (Top tier)**

For **mission-critical** workloads.

### **Features**

- Everything from Enterprise On-Ramp
- **Designated Technical Account Manager (Dedicated TAM)**
- Access to **AWS Incident Detection & Response** (extra fee)
- Highest level of architectural + operational support

### **Response Times**

- Production impaired: < 4 hours
- Production system down: < 1 hour
- **Business-critical system down: < 15 minutes** ← *fastest SLA*

### **Use Case**

- Mission-critical 24/7 workloads
- Enterprises requiring continuous engagement with AWS
- Compliance-heavy environments needing guaranteed SLAs

## **Exam Summary Table**

| Support Plan | TA Checks | Contact Method | Key Addons | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Basic** | 7 core | None | PH Dashboard | Learning & testing |
| **Developer** | 7 core | Email (business hours) | – | Small dev teams |
| **Business** | **Full TA** | 24/7 phone/email/chat | IEM (extra) | Production workloads |
| **Enterprise On-Ramp** | Full TA | 24/7 | Pool of TAMs, Concierge | Business-critical |
| **Enterprise** | Full TA | 24/7 | **Dedicated TAM**, IDR | Mission-critical |

---

## **Most Common Exam Triggers**

- “**Production workload** needs fast response” → **Business Support**
- “**Business-critical workloads**” → **Enterprise On-Ramp**
- “**Mission-critical, need <15 min SLA**” → **Enterprise Support**
- “Need **full Trusted Advisor checks**” → Business / Enterprise
- “Need **TAM** support”
    - Pool of TAMs → **Enterprise On-Ramp**
    - Dedicated TAM → **Enterprise**
